# Fact-check: Register for a summer camp

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/work-employment/register-summer-camp>
- **Source file:** `src/content/register-summer-camp.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-29
- **Summary:** 15 claims reviewed — 7 verified, 3 discrepant, 5 unverifiable. Average certainty: **71%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **"Registration opens in May 2026" is still unconfirmed and likely wrong.** As of 2026-05-29, no 2026 summer camp registration announcement exists on youthaffairs.gov.bb, GIS, or Ministry of Youth social media. The most recent cycle (2025) announced registration on 28 June 2025 with a 15 July deadline and camps running 21 July – 22 August. Today is 29 May 2026 and registration has not opened. Parents reading this line expect action now — the claim is discrepant and high-stakes.
2. **"Volunteer recruitment begins around April" contradicted by 2025 cycle.** The Division of Youth's own 2025 volunteer recruitment post is dated 28 June 2025 — not April. The youthaffairs.gov.bb homepage currently shows "Calling All Volunteers: Join the National Summer Camp Programme 2025!" as its most recent camp-related news, confirming late June as the pattern. This claim should be corrected.
3. **"Sports" as a specialty camp type is not corroborated.** The Ministry of Youth lists specialty camps as entrepreneurship (YES Experience Enterprise), digital media/film (Next Steps Training Initiative), cultural arts, and special needs. "Sports" is not enumerated as a distinct specialty camp type; sports activities are embedded in the general multi-activity programme. "Digital media" is documented but absent from the page.
4. **"Research Department" is an unverified organisational name.** No public source (youthaffairs.gov.bb/about-2/, gov.bb, or mysce.gov.bb) lists a "Research Department" within the Division of Youth Affairs. No such department name appears in any confirmed organogram or staff listing.
5. **Contact details remain absent.** The Division of Youth Affairs publishes phone `(246) 535-3835`, email `YDP@barbados.gov.bb`, and a physical address (Sky Mall, Haggatt Hall, St. Michael). None appear on the content page. Volunteers can also reach Youth Projects Coordinator David Denny at 535-3852 or mysce.youthprojects@barbados.gov.bb.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

- **Ministry contact details missing.** The Division of Youth Affairs publishes contact information that would help citizens who cannot register online, or who want to confirm 2026 registration dates before they are announced:
  - Phone: `(246) 535-3835` (Division of Youth Affairs)
  - Email: `YDP@barbados.gov.bb` (for completed paper forms)
  - Physical address: Sky Mall, Haggatt Hall, St. Michael
  - For volunteers: David Denny (Youth Projects Coordinator) at 535-3852 or `mysce.youthprojects@barbados.gov.bb`
  - Source: [Ministry of Youth — About the National Summer Camp Programme](https://youthaffairs.gov.bb/about-national-summer-camps/); [Division of Youth Affairs — Calling All Volunteers 2025](https://youthaffairs.gov.bb/calling-all-volunteers-join-the-national-summer-camp-programme-2025/)

- **"Digital media" is a named specialty camp type.** The page currently lists "cultural arts, sports and entrepreneurship." Based on youthaffairs.gov.bb, "digital media" (Next Steps Training Initiative Digital Media and Film) is a documented specialty camp type useful for citizens looking for this option.

- **Two special needs programmes, not one.** The page says "a summer camp for children who have special needs." The Ministry documents two special needs programmes — the plural may be worth noting.

- **2026 registration timing: no announcement yet.** As of 2026-05-29, the Division of Youth Affairs has not published a 2026 summer camp registration announcement. Based on the 2025 cycle (registration announced late June 2025), the page should either remove the specific "May 2026" line or replace it with a note directing citizens to check youthaffairs.gov.bb for the announcement.

- **Volunteer training timing likely July, not June.** Given 2025 recruitment was announced late June and camps started 21 July, pre-camp training almost certainly occurred in early-to-mid July, not June as the page states.
