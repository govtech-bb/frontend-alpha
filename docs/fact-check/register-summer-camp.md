# Fact-check: Register for a summer camp

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/work-employment/register-summer-camp>
- **Source file:** `src/content/register-summer-camp.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-28
- **Summary:** 14 claims reviewed — 7 verified, 3 discrepant, 4 unverifiable. Average certainty: **72%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **"Registration opens in May 2026" cannot be confirmed and is likely stale.** No 2026-specific registration announcement exists on youthaffairs.gov.bb, gisbarbados.gov.bb, or the Division of Youth Affairs Facebook page as of 2026-05-28. The most recent documented cycle (2025) announced registration on 28 June 2025 with a 15 July deadline — not in May. This claim is discrepant and high-stakes for parents trying to act on it today.
2. **"Volunteer recruitment begins around April" is contradicted by the 2025 cycle.** The Division of Youth's own Facebook post announcing 2025 volunteer recruitment is dated 28 June 2025 — not April. No authoritative source places recruitment opening in April. This claim is discrepant and should be corrected or removed.
3. **"Sports" listed as a specialty camp type is not corroborated.** The Ministry of Youth lists specialised camps as entrepreneurship (YES Experience Enterprise), cultural arts, and digital media. "Sports" as a distinct specialty camp type is not documented; sports activities are embedded in the general multi-activity programme.
4. **"Research Department" is an unverified department name.** The Division of Youth Affairs does not list a "Research Department" by that name in any public directory. A Research Officer role exists, but no separate department. The term should be confirmed with the agency or reworded.
5. **Contact details remain absent from the page.** The Ministry of Youth publishes phone `(246) 535-3835`, email `YDP@barbados.gov.bb`, and a physical address (Sky Mall, Haggatt Hall, St. Michael). None appear on the content page.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

- **Ministry contact details missing.** The Division of Youth Affairs publishes the following contact information which would help citizens who cannot register online, or who want to confirm 2026 registration dates before they are announced:
  - Phone: `(246) 535-3835` (Division of Youth Affairs)
  - Email: `YDP@barbados.gov.bb` (for completed paper forms)
  - Physical address: Sky Mall, Haggatt Hall, St. Michael
  - Source: [Ministry of Youth — About the National Summer Camp Programme](https://youthaffairs.gov.bb/about-national-summer-camps/)

- **"Digital media" is a named specialty camp type.** The page currently lists "cultural arts, sports and entrepreneurship." Based on youthaffairs.gov.bb, "digital media" (Next Steps Training Initiative Digital Media and Film) is a documented specialty camp type and would be useful for citizens looking for this option.

- **Two special needs programmes, not one.** The page says "a summer camp for children who have special needs." The Ministry documents two special needs programmes. The plural may be worth noting.

- **2026 registration timing: no announcement yet.** As of 2026-05-28, the Division of Youth Affairs has not published a 2026 summer camp registration announcement. Based on the 2025 cycle (registration announced 28 June 2025), the page should either remove the specific "May 2026" line or replace it with a note directing citizens to check youthaffairs.gov.bb for the announcement.
