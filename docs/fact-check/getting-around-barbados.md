# Fact-check: Getting around Barbados

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/travel-id-citizenship/getting-around-barbados>
- **Source file:** `src/content/getting-around-barbados.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-28
- **Summary:** 13 claims reviewed — 7 verified, 3 discrepant, 3 unverifiable. Average certainty: **68%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **Entire visitor driving permit section is obsolete.** The Barbados Revenue Authority abolished visitor driving permits effective 15 October 2025. Visitors may now drive in Barbados on a valid home-country licence without any permit or fee. The Car Rental Levy (BBD $5/day, max $35 per rental contract) is now collected by rental companies directly — it is not a citizen-facing permit application. Every paragraph, fee, and procedure under "Obtaining a Drivers Permit" is wrong today. Citizens reading this page will waste time applying for a permit that no longer exists or, worse, believe they cannot drive legally without one.

2. **ZR routes are not "numbered one through eleven."** The Transport Authority's official route list at ta.gov.bb shows 31 route-taxi routes, including numbered routes up to Route 61 and alphabetical variants (1A, 1B, 3C, 3D, 3L, 3O, etc.). The "1 through 11" description is an outdated characterisation that first appeared on the gov.bb source page itself (likely pre-2015) and has been copied forward unchanged.

3. **The `source_url` page (`gov.bb/Visit-Barbados/getting-around-barbados`) is live but itself carries the abolished-permit information**, so it cannot be used to justify retaining the permit section. Both the alpha page and its declared source are simultaneously wrong on the same facts.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

- **Car Rental Levy (BBD $5/day, max $35)** — since visitor permits were abolished, the only transport-related cost for visiting drivers renting a car is the Car Rental Levy built into rental rates. Citizens would benefit from knowing this so they can verify it appears on their rental invoice.
- **Exact-change requirement for Transport Board buses** — the fare policy page explicitly states no change is given. Visitors unfamiliar with cash-only exact-fare systems may be left at the bus stop without the right coins.
- **School-uniform / senior fare exemptions** — Transport Board buses are free for school children in uniform (under 18) and for old-age pensioners. These are not mentioned on the page and are relevant to visiting families.
- **"Taxies" is a misspelling** — line 42 of the source file reads "Most Taxies". Should be "Taxis". Not a fact error, but a proofing issue on a citizen-facing page.
- **Transport Authority website (ta.gov.bb)** — the Transport Authority (a separate body from the Transport Board) operates the official route-and-schedule database for all PSVs and ZRs. Linking to ta.gov.bb/Routes/ would give visitors better route-finding capability than the Transport Board's bus-focused tool.
