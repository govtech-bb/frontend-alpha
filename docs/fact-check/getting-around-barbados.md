# Fact-check: Getting around Barbados

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/travel-id-citizenship/getting-around-barbados>
- **Source file:** `src/content/getting-around-barbados.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-29
- **Summary:** 15 claims reviewed — 8 verified, 4 discrepant, 3 unverifiable. Average certainty: **71%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **Entire visitor driving permit section is obsolete.** The Barbados Revenue Authority abolished visitor driving permits effective 15 October 2025. Visitors may now drive in Barbados on a valid home-country licence without any permit or fee. The Car Rental Levy (BBD $5/day, max $35 per rental contract) is collected by rental companies directly — it is not a citizen-facing permit application. Every paragraph, fee, and procedure under "Obtaining a Drivers Permit" is wrong today. Citizens reading this page will waste time applying for a permit that no longer exists or, worse, believe they cannot drive legally without one. (Status unchanged from 2026-05-28 pass — the page has not been corrected.)

2. **A sibling page `visitor-permit-application` on alpha.gov.bb is also obsolete and still live.** The protected page at `https://alpha.gov.bb/visitor-permit-application` still presents the full online application for a Visitor's Driving Permit, including the $10/$100 fee tiers and SurePay payment instructions. Two pages on the same alpha site now contradict the BRA policy. The sibling page is a new finding in this pass.

3. **ZR routes are not "numbered one through eleven."** The Transport Authority's official route list at ta.gov.bb now shows 42 route-taxi routes (updated from 31 noted in prior pass), including numbered routes up to Route 61 and 16 lettered sub-routes (1A, 1B, 1D, 3C, 3D, 3E, 3L, 3M, 3N, 3O, 3Q, 3R, 3V, 3W, 8B). The "1 through 11" description is an outdated characterisation that is more inaccurate than the prior pass determined.

4. **The `source_url` page (`gov.bb/Visit-Barbados/getting-around-barbados`) is live but itself carries the abolished-permit information**, so it cannot be used to justify retaining the permit section. Both the alpha page and its declared source are simultaneously wrong on the same facts.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

- **Car Rental Levy (BBD $5/day, max $35)** — since visitor permits were abolished, the only transport-related cost for visiting drivers renting a car is the Car Rental Levy built into rental rates. Citizens would benefit from knowing this so they can verify it appears on their rental invoice. Confirmed by [BRA — Car Rental Levy Guide and Resources](https://bra.gov.bb/News/Announcements/Car-Rental-Levy-Guide-and-Resources).
- **Exact-change requirement for Transport Board buses** — the fare policy page explicitly states no change is given. Visitors unfamiliar with cash-only exact-fare systems may be left at the bus stop without the right coins. Confirmed by [Transport Board — Fare Policy](https://www.transportboard.com/about-us/fare-policy/).
- **School-uniform / senior fare exemptions** — Transport Board buses are free for school children in uniform (under 18) and for old-age pensioners. These are not mentioned on the page and are relevant to visiting families.
- **"Taxies" is a misspelling** — line 41 of the source file reads "Most Taxies". Should be "Taxis". Not a fact error, but a proofing issue on a citizen-facing page.
- **Transport Authority website (ta.gov.bb)** — the Transport Authority operates the official route-and-schedule database for all PSVs and ZRs. Linking to [ta.gov.bb/Routes/](https://ta.gov.bb/Routes/) would give visitors better route-finding capability for ZRs and minibuses beyond the Transport Board's bus-focused tool.
- **42 ZR routes, not 11** — the TA now lists 42 route-taxi routes; even the "31 routes" figure from the prior pass was an undercount. The actual network has grown.
