# Fact-check: Register a death

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/family-birth-relationships/register-a-death>
- **Source file:** `src/content/register-a-death.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-28
- **Summary:** 9 claims reviewed — 5 verified, 2 discrepant, 2 unverifiable. Average certainty: **79%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **Page conflates death registration with death certificate applications — the actual registration process is missing.** The page is titled "Register a death" but its content describes how to apply for a death certificate copy, not how to register a death (i.e., give Notice of Death). Under the Vital Statistics Registration Act Cap. 192A, a Notice of Death must be given within five (5) days of death, and the Funeral Director in charge of the body is responsible for registering the death. None of this appears on the page. Citizens looking for guidance on their legal obligation after a death will not find it here; they will only find certificate-copy ordering instructions. This is the most significant content gap in the entire page.

2. **"New Supreme Court Complex White Park Road" uses the two-word spelling "White Park Road".** The authoritative public spelling used by barbadoslawcourts.gov.bb and gov.bb/Departments/registration is one word: "Whitepark Road". The source URL (gov.bb/Citizens/register-death and gov.bb/register-death) also uses "White Park Road" (two words), so the error may be upstream. All other fact-checked pages in this family (`register-a-birth`, `get-birth-certificate`) use the single-word spelling. For consistency with verified sources and other alpha.gov.bb pages, "Whitepark Road" should be used.

3. **$5.00 mail application Money Order is correct only for a standard certificate copy; if the applicant also wants a Cause of Death certificate, a separate $10.00 Money Order is required.** The page instructs mail applicants to include "a Money Order in the amount of BDS$5.00" without noting the different fee for the Cause of Death certificate. A citizen mailing an application for a Cause of Death certificate with only $5.00 would have their application rejected or face delays.

4. **No contact email on the page.** The Registration Department's email address `registrarsupremecourt@barbados.gov.bb` is confirmed on barbadoslawcourts.gov.bb and is listed on the companion `get-birth-certificate` and `get-death-certificate` alpha.gov.bb pages. Its absence here is a gap, particularly for overseas applicants who cannot call during Barbados business hours.

5. **A February 2025 announcement proposes transferring death registration responsibility to the Electoral and Boundaries Commission (EBC).** This has not yet taken effect (as of 2026-05-28), but if implemented it would change where and how deaths are registered. The page should note the current state clearly and be flagged for re-verification once the transfer is formalised.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

- **5-day Notice of Death deadline and funeral director's responsibility** — confirmed on [Barbados Judicial System — Registration of Deaths](https://www.barbadoslawcourts.gov.bb/useful-links/for-public/services/vital-statistics-registration/registration-of-deaths). The page currently only covers how to obtain a certificate copy; the legal obligation to give Notice of Death is entirely absent. This is the most consequential missing content.

- **Required documents for death registration** — barbadoslawcourts.gov.bb lists: Medical Certificate of Death, Notice of Death, identification document of the deceased. None of these are mentioned on the page.

- **Email address** `registrarsupremecourt@barbados.gov.bb` — confirmed on [Barbados Judicial System — Certificates](https://www.barbadoslawcourts.gov.bb/useful-links/for-public/how-to-order/certificates). It is listed on the companion `get-birth-certificate` page and the `get-death-certificate` page, but is absent here. Overseas applicants in particular would benefit from this contact.

- **Money Order amount for mail applications should vary by certificate type** — the page hardcodes BDS$5.00 in the mail-application instructions but the Cause of Death certificate costs $10.00. The barbadoslawcourts.gov.bb Certificates page correctly says "a Money Order for the relevant processing fee" (unspecified amount). The page should either say "for the relevant processing fee" or specify both amounts.

- **February 2025 proposal to transfer death registrations to the Electoral and Boundaries Commission (EBC)** — Attorney General Dale Marshall announced in February 2025 that the EBC would "hopefully, very soon" assume responsibility for birth and death registrations. As of 2026-05-28 this transfer has not yet taken effect, but the page should be flagged for re-verification once the change is implemented. See [Barbados Today — Electoral commission to take over birth, death registrations (2025-02-19)](https://barbadostoday.bb/2025/02/19/electoral-commission-to-take-over-birth-death-registrations-ag/).

- **Overlap with `get-death-certificate` page** — the alpha.gov.bb page `get-death-certificate` at `https://alpha.gov.bb/family-birth-relationships/get-death-certificate` covers essentially the same certificate-copy process. The two pages should be reconciled so "register a death" covers the legal notification process and "get a death certificate" covers obtaining copies.
