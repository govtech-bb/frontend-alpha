# Fact-check: Apply for a position as a temporary teacher

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/work-employment/apply-for-a-position-as-a-temporary-teacher>
- **Source file:** `src/content/apply-for-a-position-as-a-temporary-teacher.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-28
- **Summary:** 13 claims reviewed — 8 verified, 0 discrepant, 4 unverifiable (incl. primary-school scope, CSEC requirements, form nav path, form scope). Average certainty: **76%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **"Primary school only" may be too narrow.** The page restricts to "a government-funded primary school in Barbados", but the seamlessdocs application form is titled generically "Application for Temporary Teacher" (not primary only), and the Ministry of Public Service teacher circular covers appointments across both primary and secondary levels. If the page is meant only for primary-school applicants this should be confirmed with the Ministry of Educational Transformation; if it covers secondary too, the opening sentence needs updating.

2. **CSEC eligibility — 5 subjects including science: unverified.** The page states applicants need "at least 5 CXC subjects at CSEC general proficiency level, including English language, maths and a science subject." A Ministry of Public Service circular for teacher posts (dated June 2025) was located but its PDF was not machine-readable; a third-party summary of that circular referenced 4 subjects (not 5) and English (not maths or science) for the Teacher Special Grade. The science-subject requirement could not be confirmed or refuted from a publicly readable primary source. This must be verified with the Ministry.

3. **`mes.gov.bb` 301 redirects to `education.gov.bb`.** The page tells citizens to look for updates on "the Ministry of Educational Transformation … website." The official domain listed on gov.bb is `mes.gov.bb`, but that now silently forwards all pages to `education.gov.bb`. Deep-linked URLs (e.g. the original application-form announcement at `mes.gov.bb/News/Latest/Application-Form-for-Temporary-Teachers.aspx`) redirect to a 404. Citizens following stale links from the ministry's own site will hit dead pages. This is a Ministry IT issue; the alpha.gov.bb content could link directly to the form URL or to the resources page on `education.gov.bb`.

4. **Police Certificate of Character fee BBD $20: confirmed.** This resolves the pending item in `_inventory.md`. The fee is confirmed at BBD $20 by the official `forms.gov.bb/CertificateOfCharacter` application page and corroborated by multiple GIS articles.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

**Contact email for the Ministry.** Citizens with questions about the temporary teacher application currently have no contact detail on the page. The MPS circular PDF includes the address teachervacancy@mes.gov.bb for teacher-vacancy inquiries, and the Ministry's general email is info@mes.gov.bb / (246) 535-0600. Adding one of these would reduce friction for applicants who are unsure whether they qualify.

**CARICOM Skills Certificate pathway not mentioned.** CARICOM nationals who do not yet have a "right to work" in Barbados can obtain one by applying to the Barbados Accreditation Council for a Certificate of Recognition of CARICOM Skills Qualifications (bac.gov.bb). This requires a minimum 7 working days. The page currently implies CARICOM nationals either already have the right to work or do not — it does not point them to the pathway for obtaining it.

**"Recent" Police Certificate of Character — validity period not defined.** The page says "a recent Police Certificate of Character" without specifying what "recent" means. The Royal Barbados Police Force (RBPF) typically regards a certificate as valid for 6 months, but this is not stated on the page. Candidates who obtain one at application stage and are not interviewed until months later may need a new one.
