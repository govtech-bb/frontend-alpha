# Fact-check: Get disaster relief assistance

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/money-financial-support/get-disaster-relief-assistance>
- **Source file:** `src/content/get-disaster-relief-assistance.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-29
- **Summary:** 14 claims reviewed — 7 verified, 3 discrepant, 4 unverifiable. Average certainty: **74%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **"Welfare Department" is the wrong agency name.** The Welfare Department was dissolved on 2 January 2026 and merged into the **Social Empowerment Agency (SEA)**, together with the Child Care Board, National Assistance Board, National Disabilities Unit, and the Resilience and Reintegration Unit. Every occurrence of "Welfare Department" on this page (lines 21, 38, 44, 75, 76, 96) is now an out-of-date agency name. Citizens presenting at the old "Welfare Department" will be redirected; the page should use "Social Empowerment Agency" and link to the new client centres.

2. **Both the agency name and address are wrong — citizens will go to the wrong building.** The page lists "Welfare Department, Perry Gap, Bridgetown" (lines 22–24 and 40–43). The agency is now the **Social Empowerment Agency (SEA)**, and its confirmed main office is at **4th Floor, Warrens Office Complex, Warrens, St. Michael** (phone: (246) 310-1600/01/02). The old Welfare Department was at Weymouth Corporate Centre, Roebuck Street — not Perry Gap — and that address is now also superseded. "Perry Gap" appears on no Tier 1 or Tier 2 source. A citizen travelling to "Perry Gap" during or after a disaster will not find the SEA.

3. **Welfare Department phone number is wrong.** Lines 25 and 44 list `+1 246-535-1000`, which is the former Welfare Department's PBX. The SEA's published number is **(246) 310-1600/01/02**. Citizens who dial 535-1000 may still reach legacy staff, but this number is now the old department. The page should be updated to the SEA contact.

4. **Missing DEM phone number.** The DEM address appears on the page (lines 80–84) but no phone number is given. The confirmed DEM contact is **(246) 438-7575** (confirmed on both dem.gov.bb and gov.bb). For a disaster-related page, this is a significant omission — citizens in a crisis need to be able to call the DEM directly.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

- **SEA client centres.** The Social Empowerment Agency now operates new client centres (Six Roads, St Philip; Southern Plaza, Oistins, Christ Church as of March 2026; additional centres planned across all parishes). Citizens may be better directed to their nearest SEA centre than to the Warrens head office.

- **DEM phone number missing.** The DEM address appears on the page (lines 80–84) but no phone number is given. The confirmed DEM contact is **(246) 438-7575** (confirmed on [dem.gov.bb/contact](https://dem.gov.bb/contact) and [gov.bb](https://www.gov.bb/Departments/emergency-management)). For a disaster-related page, this is a significant omission.

- **DEM email missing.** DEM's published email is `deminfo@barbados.gov.bb` — useful for citizens seeking further guidance outside business hours.

- **30 District Emergency Organisations.** The page names DEOs as a submission channel but does not help citizens locate their local DEO. DEM's website at `dem.gov.bb/deo` lists all 30 DEOs with contact details — a useful cross-link.

- **Agency name change note.** A brief parenthetical "(formerly the Welfare Department)" would help citizens who know the old name find the new agency.
