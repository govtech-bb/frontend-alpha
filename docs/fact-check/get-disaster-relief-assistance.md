# Fact-check: Get disaster relief assistance

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/money-financial-support/get-disaster-relief-assistance>
- **Source file:** `src/content/get-disaster-relief-assistance.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-28
- **Summary:** 14 claims reviewed — 7 verified, 3 discrepant, 4 unverifiable. Average certainty: **74%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **"Welfare Department" is the wrong agency name.** The Welfare Department was dissolved on 2 January 2026 and merged into the **Social Empowerment Agency (SEA)**, together with the Child Care Board, National Assistance Board, National Disabilities Unit, and the Resilience and Reintegration Unit. Every occurrence of "Welfare Department" on this page (lines 21, 38, 44, 75, 76, 96) is now an out-of-date agency name. Citizens presenting at the old "Welfare Department" will be redirected; the page should use "Social Empowerment Agency" and link to the new client centres.

2. **Welfare Department address is wrong.** The page lists "Perry Gap, Bridgetown, Saint Michael" as the Welfare Department address (lines 22–24 and 40–43). All authoritative government sources (gov.bb, the 2017 GIS telephone directory, govserv.org) give the head office as **Weymouth Corporate Centre, Roebuck Street, Bridgetown, St. Michael**. "Perry Gap" appears only in one Tier 4 listing site (Totally Barbados) with no corroboration. This is a significant citizen-impact error — a citizen could turn up at the wrong location during a disaster emergency.

3. **Welfare Department phone number is almost certainly wrong.** Lines 25 and 44 list `+1 246-535-1000`. While 535-1000 is a real Welfare Department / SEA number, this is formatted identically to the partially-wrong phone on the sister "Apply for financial assistance" page (already logged as F-00E). The published numbers are `(246) 535-1000` and `(246) 535-1023`; no single number is a complete contact. The page should list both, or confirm the SEA's new published number.

4. **District Emergency Organisations are named incorrectly as "District Emergency Organizations"** — minor but worth correcting to the DEM's official plural spelling "Organisations" (British English, consistent with all Barbados government usage).

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

- **SEA client centres.** The Social Empowerment Agency now operates new client centres (Six Roads, St Philip; Southern Plaza, Oistins, Christ Church as of March 2026; additional St Michael centres planned). Citizens may be better directed to the nearest SEA centre than to the legacy Weymouth Corporate Centre address.

- **DEM phone number missing.** The DEM address appears on the page (lines 80–84) but no phone number is given. The published DEM contact is `(246) 438-7575` (confirmed on dem.gov.bb and gov.bb). For a disaster-related page, this is a significant omission.

- **DEM email missing.** DEM's published email is `deminfo@barbados.gov.bb` — useful for citizens seeking further guidance.

- **30 District Emergency Organisations.** The page names DEOs as a submission channel but does not help citizens locate their local DEO. DEM's website at `dem.gov.bb/deo` lists all 30 DEOs with contact details — a useful cross-link.

- **Agency name change note.** A brief parenthetical "(formerly the Welfare Department)" would help citizens who know the old name find the new agency.
