# Fact-check: Request a Presidential Visit for a Centenarian

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/family-birth-relationships/request-a-presidential-visit-for-a-centenarian>
- **Source file:** `src/content/request-a-presidential-visit-for-a-centenarian/index.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-28
- **Summary:** 9 claims reviewed — 3 verified, 1 discrepant, 5 unverifiable. Average certainty: **58%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **President's formal title is wrong.** The page identifies the President as "Lieutenant Colonel Jeffrey Bostic". His formal presidential title — used consistently by GIS, CBC, and all official sources since 30 November 2025 — is "The Most Honourable Jeffrey Bostic" or, in longer form, "Lieutenant Colonel the Most Honourable Jeffrey Bostic." Using the military rank alone without the presidential honorific, while not entirely inaccurate, does not match any official presentation of the current President's title on a Government of Barbados page.

2. **"3 months before the birthday" advance-notice requirement is unverifiable.** No Tier 1 or Tier 2 source documents this specific lead time. The Office of the President website returns no substantive procedural content, and GIS articles covering centenarian visits are descriptive only. If the requirement is internally set by the Office, citizens relying on this figure have no independent way to verify it — and if the real window is shorter or longer, citizens could miss the opportunity entirely.

3. **All document requirements are unverifiable.** The requirements for an original birth certificate, an original marriage certificate (for female centenarians who are or have been married), and the affidavit provision appear on no Government of Barbados public source. They may reflect internal Office of the President practice, but cannot be confirmed from the public web.

4. **No contact information is provided.** The page lists only a postal address. The Office of the President publishes a phone number and email address through presidentofbarbados.org and secondary sources; these are missing from the page. For a time-sensitive request (the 3-month window), phone or email is more reliable than post.

5. **No `source_url` is declared in `content-directory.ts`.** The page has no declared government source URL against which the content can be compared. There is no formal government page on alpha.gov.bb's current parent site (gov.bb) for this service, so the absence is understandable — but it means all content on this page is single-sourced from the alpha.gov.bb team.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

**Missing contact details for the Office of the President.** The page provides only a postal address. Multiple sources confirm that the Office of the President has phone and email contacts that would be more practical for a time-sensitive request with a stated 3-month advance notice requirement:

- presidentofbarbados.org is the official website but returned no phone/email content during this check
- A secondary source (presidentofbarbados.org via search result snippet) cites phone numbers (246) 431-2200, (246) 429-6652, and (246) 228-0838, and email barbados@foreign.gov.bb — however, the foreign.gov.bb email address appears to belong to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Foreign Trade, not the Office of the President, and cannot be verified as a direct Office of the President contact

**Recommendation:** The GovBB team should obtain and add verified contact details (phone and email) for the Office of the President from the Office directly. Given the service involves a personal visit by the head of state and has a strict lead-time, providing a direct point of contact is important for citizen confidence.

**gov.bb/Visit-Barbados/president is stale.** The official gov.bb page for the President was still displaying the profile of Dame Sandra Mason (the previous President) as of 2026-05-28. Jeffrey Bostic took office on 30 November 2025. This is a separate finding from this page but worth flagging to the GovBB team for escalation to the Cabinet Office or Office of the President.
