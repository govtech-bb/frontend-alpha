## Headline issues for triage

1. **bdsfinance.gov.bb and economicaffairs.gov.bb both return ECONNREFUSED (Claim 14).** Both outbound URLs — the CTA button linking to `https://www.economicaffairs.gov.bb` and the `ministries.ts` website entry `https://bdsfinance.gov.bb/` — are unreachable as at 2026-05-29 (re-confirmed from 2026-05-28). Citizens clicking either link receive a browser error. **Tier A — citizen-facing broken links; fix immediately.**

2. **Parliamentary Secretary Senator Jepter Ince is not in the 2026–2031 Senate (Claims 9, 22).** The page lists "Senator Jepter Ince" as Parliamentary Secretary for Economic Affairs at two locations (lines 80, 142). The Barbados Parliament website confirms Ince is not among the 23 senators for the current 2026–2031 term. The number (535-1304) may still be a live office line, but the name is wrong. **Tier A — fix immediately.**

3. **PS Economic Affairs is listed as Annette Weekes; current PS is Jennifer Hunte (Claim 7).** The Administration table names "Mrs. Annette Weekes Ag." at line 95. The gov.bb PS grades page (re-confirmed 2026-05-29) lists "Mrs. Jennifer Hunte — Permanent Secretary (Economic Affairs and Investment) (AG)". Annette Weekes is absent from the current PS grades list. **Tier A — fix immediately.**

4. **Director of Research Patrick McCaskie has moved to a different ministry (Claim 10).** Line 113 lists McCaskie as Director of Research for Economic Affairs at ext. 535-1306. The PS grades page (re-confirmed 2026-05-29) shows McCaskie as "Permanent Secretary (AG), Ministry of Training and Tertiary Education". He was promoted and moved; the Research Unit listing is outdated. **Tier A — fix immediately.**

5. **Truncated phone number for Secretary (ag) Heather Alleyne-Prescott (Claim 8).** The number "535-131" at line 96 is a 6-digit fragment — a valid Barbados extension is 7 digits. The number is undialable. **Tier B — correct the typo.**

6. **New finding: PM Mottley holds "Economic Affairs and Development" in 2026–2031 cabinet (Claim 23).** The current cabinet (gov.bb/cabinet.php, re-confirmed 2026-05-29) shows PM Mottley as "Minister of Economic Affairs and Development" while Straughn is "Minister of Finance". The content page treats Economic Affairs as Straughn's portfolio. The ministry's institutional name is unchanged, but the political oversight split has changed and may affect how the Parliamentary Secretary line (535-1304) is described. **Tier B — verify attribution with ministry.**
