## Headline issues for triage

1. **Parliamentary Secretary Senator Jepter Ince is no longer in the Senate (lines 79, 142).** The page lists "Senator Jepter Ince" as Parliamentary Secretary for Economic Affairs. Ince is not a member of the current 2026–2031 Senate (sworn in after the February 2026 general election). The role should be either removed or updated to reflect any current appointment. Citizens calling the listed number (535-1304) for Parliamentary Secretary matters will reach an office whose occupant may have changed. **Tier A — fix immediately.**

2. **PS Economic Affairs is wrong — Annette Weekes has moved on (line 95).** The Administration table lists "Mrs. Annette Weekes Ag." as PS Economic Affairs. The current Permanent Secretaries list on gov.bb (as at 2026-05-28) shows **Mrs. Jennifer Hunte (Ag)** as Permanent Secretary (Economic Affairs and Investment). Annette Weekes is not listed in the current PS grades. Staff directory tables with named individuals go stale quickly; the whole named-staff section of this page requires verification with the ministry. **Tier A — fix immediately.**

3. **Director of Research Patrick McCaskie has been reassigned (line 113).** The Research Unit table lists Patrick McCaskie as Director of Research at ext. 535-1306. McCaskie now appears on the gov.bb Permanent Secretaries list as PS (Ag) for the Ministry of Training and Tertiary Education — a different ministry entirely. The GIS (March 2025) also refers to him as "Permanent Secretary in the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Investment" at that time, so the transition likely occurred in early-to-mid 2025. The Research Unit director listing is therefore outdated. **Tier A — fix immediately.**

4. **bdsfinance.gov.bb is not resolving (line 165 / ministries.ts line 450).** The outbound link to the Ministry of Finance website (`https://bdsfinance.gov.bb/`) returns ECONNREFUSED — the site is unreachable. Citizens clicking it will see a browser error. Verify whether the domain is temporarily down or permanently decommissioned, and update the link accordingly. **Tier A — citizen-facing broken link.**

5. **Truncated phone number in Administration table (line 96).** The Secretary (ag) Heather Alleyne-Prescott is listed with telephone "535-131" — this is a 6-digit fragment, not a valid Barbados number (Barbados extensions are 7 digits: 246-535-XXXX). It should read "535-131X" where X is the missing digit. The number as listed is unreachable. **Tier B — correct the typo.**

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

1. **Director General, Finance and Economic Affairs.** The gov.bb PS grades page lists a new senior position above the two PS levels: **Mr. Ian Carrington, Director General, Finance and Economic Affairs**. This is a cross-cutting co-ordination role not reflected anywhere in the content page or ministries.ts. It may be worth adding to the contact table for citizens seeking the most senior officials.

2. **bdsfinance.gov.bb is down — no working online presence.** The Ministry of Finance appears to have no functioning public website as at 2026-05-28. Both `bdsfinance.gov.bb` and `economicaffairs.gov.bb` return connection errors. Citizens are directed off alpha.gov.bb with no working destination. The GovBB team should liaise with the ministry to establish a current URL, or remove the outbound links if no site exists.

3. **Named-staff tables are inherently volatile.** This is the most staff-detail-heavy ministry page on alpha.gov.bb. It contains approximately 40 named individuals across multiple unit tables. Named staff listings go stale quickly (promotions, transfers, departures). At minimum three named individuals in the Administration and Research sections are confirmed out-of-date (Weekes, McCaskie, Ince). The page design should be reviewed — a preference for role-only tables (no names) or a mechanism for the ministry to maintain the page directly would reduce stale-data risk.
