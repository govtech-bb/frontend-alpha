# Fact-check: Terms & Conditions

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/terms-conditions>
- **Source file:** `src/content/terms-conditions.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-29
- **Summary:** 13 claims reviewed — 7 verified, 2 discrepant, 4 unverifiable. Average certainty: **71%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **Wrong ministry name — still live (F-090).** Line 29 says "Ministry of Innovation, Science and Smart Technology." The canonical name on `gov.bb/Ministries` and on the ministry's own page is **"Ministry of Industry, Innovation, Science and Technology (MIST)"**. The word "Industry" is missing and "Smart" is not part of the official name. The live page confirmed this error still appears on 2026-05-29. This is the only place on the page that names a responsible authority, so the error undermines accountability for data-subject rights.

2. **alpha.gov.bb is operated by GovTech Barbados Ltd., not directly by the ministry (F-091).** GovTech Barbados Ltd. — incorporated under the Companies Act on 18 September 2023 as a government-owned company — is the entity that built and operates alpha.gov.bb. Saying the site is "run by the Ministry" conflates policy ownership with technical operation and may mislead citizens seeking to exercise data rights or escalate a complaint.

3. **privacy@govtech.bb is unverifiable from any Tier-1 or Tier-2 source.** This is the sole contact point for data-subject rights under the Data Protection Act 2019. It does not appear on govtech.bb, gisbarbados.gov.bb, or gov.bb. Citizens cannot independently confirm the mailbox is monitored. (Claim 9)

4. **Data Protection Commission contact details are absent.** The page tells citizens they can "complain to the Data Protection Commissioner" but gives only the alpha.gov.bb privacy email. The Commission's published contact — Tel: 1 (246) 536-1200 / (246) 536-1212, 5th Floor, SSA Building, Vaucluse, St. Thomas — is not shown, leaving citizens with no fallback if the mailbox fails.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

1. **Data Protection Commission contact details not listed.** The page tells citizens they can "complain to the Data Protection Commissioner" but gives only `privacy@govtech.bb`. The Commission's own published contact details — Tel: 1 (246) 536-1200 / (246) 536-1212, 5th Floor, SSA Building, Vaucluse, St. Thomas — should appear as a fallback. Source: [gov.bb — Data Protection Commission](https://www.gov.bb/General/data-protection-commissioner).

2. **No identification of the formal data controller.** Under the Data Protection Act 2019, data subjects have the right to know the identity of the data controller. The page names a (wrong) ministry but does not state whether the legal data controller is the Government of Barbados, GovTech Barbados Ltd., or a specific Ministry. This matters for data-subject requests to be legally valid.

3. **No mention of the Electronic Transactions Act, Cap. 308B.** Given alpha.gov.bb handles legally consequential form submissions and payments, a reference to this Act (which governs the legal status of electronic contracts and submissions in Barbados) may be appropriate for completeness.
