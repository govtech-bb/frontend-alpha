# Fact-check: Terms & Conditions

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/terms-conditions>
- **Source file:** `src/content/terms-conditions.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-28
- **Summary:** 11 claims reviewed — 6 verified, 2 discrepant, 3 unverifiable. Average certainty: **72%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **Wrong ministry name (F-090).** Line 29 says "Ministry of Innovation, Science and Smart Technology." The canonical name on `gov.bb/Ministries` is **"Ministry of Industry, Innovation, Science and Technology (MIST)"**. The word "Industry" is missing and "Smart" is not part of the official name. This is the only place on the page that names a responsible authority, so the error undermines accountability.

2. **alpha.gov.bb is run by GovTech Barbados Ltd., not directly by the ministry (F-091).** GovTech Barbados Ltd. — a government-owned company incorporated 18 September 2023 — is the entity that built and operates alpha.gov.bb. Saying the site is "run by the Ministry" is imprecise and may mislead citizens who want to escalate a complaint or make a data-subject request to the correct legal data controller.

3. **privacy@govtech.bb contact address is not published on any Tier 1 source.** The email appears only on alpha.gov.bb itself. GovTech Barbados's own website publishes `recruitment@govtech.bb` but no privacy contact. Citizens cannot independently confirm that this mailbox is monitored. (Claim 9)

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

1. **Data Protection Commission contact details not listed.** The page mentions citizens can "complain to the Data Protection Commissioner" but gives only a single email address (privacy@govtech.bb) as a contact point. The Data Protection Commission's own contact details — Tel: 1 (246) 536-1200 / (246) 536-1212, 5th Floor, SSA Building, Vaucluse, St. Thomas — are not provided. Citizens who cannot reach the privacy mailbox have no published alternative.

2. **No identification of the data controller.** Under the Data Protection Act 2019, data subjects have the right to know the identity of the data controller. The page says "alpha.gov.bb is run by [the ministry]" but does not state whether the data controller is the Government of Barbados, GovTech Barbados Ltd., or the Ministry of Industry, Innovation, Science and Technology. This matters because the correct entity must be named for a data-subject request to be legally valid.

3. **No mention of the Computer Misuse Act or Electronic Transactions Act.** The parent gov.bb terms-and-conditions page is silent on these too, but given alpha.gov.bb handles online form submissions and payments, a reference to the Electronic Transactions Act, Cap. 308B (which governs the legal status of electronic contracts and submissions in Barbados) may be appropriate for completeness.
