# Fact-check: Apply for financial assistance

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/money-financial-support/apply-financial-assistance>
- **Source file:** `src/content/apply-financial-assistance.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-29
- **Summary:** 20 claims reviewed — 10 verified, 5 discrepant, 5 unverifiable. Average certainty: **76%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **"Electronic transfer of funds is not available yet" is false.** The Welfare Department's direct deposit / EFT system for National Assistance Grants went live on 21 May 2024 (GIS press release confirmed by web search). The page still tells citizens EFT is pending. This is the highest-priority correction: citizens may avoid providing bank details unnecessarily. Additionally, since April 2026, welfare recipients also receive a $100/month Cost of Living Cash Credit via direct deposit — reinforcing that EFT is now standard for this cohort.

2. **Speightstown office building name is wrong.** The page says "Republic Bank Building, Chapel St." — every government and GIS source calls it the "BNB Building" in Speightstown. The GIS 2017 telephone directory explicitly names the Speightstown welfare office at "BNB Building". Update to "BNB Building, Chapel Street, Speightstown, Saint Peter".

3. **Phone number format `+1 246-535-1000/16/23` is non-standard and partially wrong.** The two PBX numbers confirmed by gov.bb are `(246) 535-1000` and `(246) 535-1023`. The notation `/16/` appears nowhere in any official source — "16" is not a recognisable extension format. The GIS May 2024 direct deposit press release also lists a third number: `535-1005`. Rewrite as `(246) 535-1000`, `(246) 535-1005`, `(246) 535-1023`.

4. **Typo in section heading.** Line 33 reads "How to apply for financial **assitance**" — "assitance" is misspelled (should be "assistance").

5. **Post Office cheque arrangement may be obsolete.** The page still says citizens without a fixed address can "make arrangements through the Post Office to receive your cheque." With EFT live since May 2024 and the Cost of Living Cash Credit paying via direct bank deposit from April 2026, the cheque-via-Post-Office option may no longer be current. Needs agency confirmation.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

- **EFT now live — banking form and accounts email.** Since May 2024, citizens with approved grants receive payment by direct deposit. Citizens who do not have a bank account are helped by the Welfare Department to acquire one at no cost. The online banking details form is at `is.gd/WelfareBenefitsForm` (per GIS May 2024 release); email for banking queries: `welfare.accounts@barbados.gov.bb`. The page should reflect this.

- **Cost of Living Cash Credit (April 2026).** From 20 April 2026, welfare recipients also receive $100/month via direct bank deposit or cheque through the Cost of Living Cash Credit programme (running until March 2027). This is a significant new benefit for the page's target audience and is not mentioned. Source: [GIS — Barbados Government Launches Cost Of Living Cash Credit](https://gisbarbados.gov.bb/blog/barbados-government-launches-cost-of-living-cash-credit/).

- **Speightstown/St. James branch phone numbers.** The 2017 GIS telephone directory lists: St. Peter welfare office in BNB Building, Speightstown: `(246) 535-1049`; St. James welfare office (also in BNB Building): `(246) 535-1048`. These direct lines for the northern district may be useful for citizens who would otherwise call Bridgetown.

- **Country Road office landmark.** GIS sources confirm this office is "opposite the National Housing Corporation" — a useful wayfinding detail currently omitted.
