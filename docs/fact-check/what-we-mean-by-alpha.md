## Headline issues for triage

1. **"Alpha" terminology is consistent with international GDS practice but not defined for citizens.** The page correctly uses "alpha" in the same sense as the UK Government Digital Service and Australian Digital Transformation Agency frameworks (an experimental early-version phase where ideas are tested before building the full service). The description "early version … test ideas … learn what works" maps directly to GOV.UK and digital.gov.au definitions. No correction is needed, but the absence of any operator attribution (e.g. GovTech Barbados Ltd.) means citizens have no named accountable body — a gap also flagged on the related terms-conditions page (F-091).

2. **`alpha.gov.bb/feedback` link is live and working.** The feedback link in line 11 resolves correctly to a page titled "Help us improve alpha.gov.bb" with a working form. No issue.

3. **`publish_date: 2025-11-12` is an internal content field, not displayed to citizens.** The date cannot be independently verified as accurate against any public record. The public launch of alpha.gov.bb was announced and covered in press as October 2025; the frontmatter date of 12 November 2025 likely reflects when this specific page was added to the CMS. No public-facing claim is made about this date so it has no citizen impact, but it is flagged as unverifiable.

(No major factual errors found in this pass. The page's low claim density — it is a short governance/meta page — means the primary risks are omissions rather than errors.)

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

1. **No operator attribution on this page.** The "What we mean by alpha" page makes no mention of GovTech Barbados Ltd. as the operator of the site. By contrast, the Terms & Conditions page (now flagged under F-091) names a ministry. Visitors arriving at this page via the "Alpha" notice on the homepage have no indication of who is responsible for the site. This is a lower-stakes gap than on the terms page — citizens are unlikely to need this for service decisions — but it is an inconsistency. See [terms-conditions.md](/home/gavin/frontend-alpha/docs/fact-check/terms-conditions.md) Claim 1 (F-091) for the parallel finding.

2. **The dynamic "Alpha services" section (rendered by `page.tsx`) is not in the markdown source.** The live page shows a list of ~33 services generated at runtime from `INFORMATION_ARCHITECTURE` in `content-directory.ts`. Because this content is not in `what-we-mean-by-alpha.md`, it was not in scope for this fact-check. Individual service page fact-checks are the appropriate venue for verifying those entries. No action needed here.

3. **No link to the Terms & Conditions page from this page.** Citizens reading "What we mean by alpha" and wanting to understand data handling, privacy, or governing law have no link to `alpha.gov.bb/terms-conditions`. Given both pages serve the same governance audience, a cross-link would improve discoverability.
