- **Source file:** `src/content/local-information.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-29
- **Summary:** 5 claims reviewed — 3 verified, 1 discrepant, 1 unverifiable. Average certainty: **72%**.

## Headline issues for triage

1. **"Global population indexes" is the wrong term.** The page says Barbados is "ranked highly in most global population indexes." "Population indexes" typically measure population size or growth — Barbados ranks approximately 186th–187th in the world by population (a very small country). The intended meaning is almost certainly quality-of-life or international development indices (e.g. HDI rank #58/189, Passport Index #22/199) — but that is not what the sentence says. This is a factual accuracy error that could mislead readers about why Barbados attracts tourism. Confidence it is wrong: **90%**.

2. **Page body is thin and largely promotional.** The two-paragraph body contains only a handful of verifiable factual assertions; the rest is promotional copy. No specific statistics, contacts, or procedural information are provided that could mislead citizens in a transactional way. The main risk is reputational (inaccurate claim about ranking position) rather than citizen-impact.

3. **No major issues found for citizen-action claims** — this page carries no fees, procedures, contact details, or eligibility rules that could misdirect a citizen trying to use a government service. The sole discrepant claim is a terminology error in descriptive copy.

### Claim 3 — "Ranked highly in most global population indexes" (line 11)

<div class="claim-block claim-block--current">
<div class="claim-block-label">Currently on the page</div>
<pre class="claim-block-content">Ranked highly in most global population indexes, this draws additional
international interest and exposure which makes Barbados the thriving tourist
attraction it is today.</pre>
</div>

<div class="claim-block claim-block--correct">
<div class="claim-block-label">Should say</div>
<pre class="claim-block-content">Ranked highly in many international development and quality-of-life indexes,
this draws additional international interest and exposure which makes Barbados
the thriving tourist attraction it is today.</pre>
</div>

- **Type:** statistic / descriptive
- **Sources:** [Barbados Statistical Service — International Indices](https://stats.gov.bb/international-indices/) — lists 17 indices and Barbados rankings; HDI rank #58/189, Passport Index #22/199, Adult Literacy Rate #15/234, Gender Development Index #12/189, Freedom in the World #13/210, Corruption Perceptions Index #30/198; [Worldometer — Barbados Population](https://www.worldometers.info/world-population/barbados-population/) — Barbados population rank approximately 186th of 237 countries/territories, confirming "population indexes" is the wrong framing.
- **Status:** discrepant
- **Certainty:** 20% (that the phrase "population indexes" is correct)
- **Confidence it's wrong:** 90%
- **Citizen impact:** LOW — this is promotional introductory copy; no citizen will act on it. The error is a credibility and accuracy issue rather than a practical misdirection risk.
- **What's wrong:** "Population indexes" are indices that rank countries by population size, growth rate, or demographic composition. Barbados has a population of approximately 282,000 — ranking around 186th–187th in the world — which is not a "high" ranking. The sentence almost certainly intends to refer to international quality-of-life, development, or competitiveness indices (e.g. HDI, Passport Index, Freedom in the World) in which Barbados does rank highly for a small Caribbean nation. The Barbados Statistical Service tracks 17 such indices at stats.gov.bb/international-indices/, and Barbados ranks in the top 5–30% globally in several of them (HDI #58/189, Literacy #15/234, Freedom #13/210). The word "population" is incorrect and should be replaced.
- **Suggested fix:** Replace "global population indexes" with "international development and quality-of-life indexes" (or simply "international indexes"). Optionally, name one or two specific examples (e.g. "ranking #58 in the UNDP Human Development Index and #22 in the Henley Passport Index") to make the claim concrete and auditable.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

**Thin content with no actionable information.** The page provides no links to BTMI (Barbados Tourism Marketing Inc.), the official visitor guide at visitbarbados.org, or any practical local information (emergency numbers, taxi services, healthcare for visitors, currency, tipping customs, etc.). The gov.bb source page at `https://www.gov.bb/Visit-Barbados/local-information` similarly offers no deeper local information links. Citizens visiting this page expecting practical local travel guidance will find only two brief promotional paragraphs. Consider either expanding the page with actionable content or relinking to the appropriate BTMI resources.

**Grammar issue — "travel destination" (singular) vs "travel destinations" (plural)** on line 10: "one of the top Caribbean travel destination" should read "one of the top Caribbean travel destinations." This is an editorial rather than factual issue but should be corrected for a professional government web presence.

**HDI rank note — source discrepancy.** The Barbados Statistical Service lists Barbados at HDI rank #58/189. The UNDP 2025 Human Development Report places Barbados at rank #69/189. The BSS figure likely reflects an earlier report edition; the most current UNDP data should be cited if the page is updated with specific index rankings.
