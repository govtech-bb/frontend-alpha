# Fact-check: National registration

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/travel-id-citizenship/national-registration>
- **Source file:** `src/content/national-registration.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-29
- **Summary:** 13 claims reviewed — 5 verified, 3 discrepant, 5 unverifiable. Average certainty: **74%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **Photo requirements are materially incomplete.** The page lists only two dress-code prohibitions (head coverings and tinted glasses). The EBC's current registration guidance — confirmed on both `ebc.gov.bb/registration-information/` and `trident.gov.bb/registering/` — adds two further restrictions: no sleeveless clothing (shoulders must be covered) and no white clothing (photos are taken against a white background). Citizens who arrive in a sleeveless white top will have their photo rejected and must rebook.

2. **Governing Act is legally stale.** The page uses the term "National Registration Number" and implies the old Statistics Act framework. The Barbados Identity Management Act 2021 replaced that Act and is now the controlling statute. The EBC FAQ page states explicitly: "The Trident ID Card complies with the Barbados Identity Management Act." The phrase "National Registration Number" is obsolete.

3. **Replacement card fee correct ($60) but context is thin.** The $60 figure is confirmed. The page omits: (a) first-time registration is free; (b) same-day expedited replacement costs $100; (c) persons 65+ are exempt from the replacement fee; (d) persons with qualifying disabilities and 16-year-olds transitioning to adult cards (when the minor card is surrendered) are also exempt. These omissions could mislead citizens about costs.

4. **Fax number is unverified.** The number (246) 535-4863 does not appear on the EBC's own contact page. The EBC lists only a phone number and email. The fax may have been decommissioned.

5. **Processing time stated in the previous report's additional findings was wrong.** The prior report noted "within 12 weeks" — the current trident.gov.bb states "within eight weeks after registration." This affected only the additional findings section, not a direct page claim.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

1. **First card is free** — the EBC's current fee schedule and trident.gov.bb both confirm: "The first new identification card will be issued free of cost." The page only mentions the $60 replacement fee; omitting the "free for first registration" fact could cause citizens to hesitate or expect a charge.

2. **Same-day expedited service** — replacement cards can be obtained same-day for $100 (confirmed by [EBC — Registration Information](https://www.ebc.gov.bb/registration-information/) and [Barbados Digital ID](https://trident.gov.bb/registering/)).

3. **Age-65+ exemption from replacement fee** — persons aged 65 and over are exempt from the $60 replacement fee (confirmed by [EBC — Registration Information](https://www.ebc.gov.bb/registration-information/) and [trident.gov.bb/registering/](https://trident.gov.bb/registering/)).

4. **16-year-olds converting from minor to adult card** — if the old minor card is returned, conversion to an adult card is free; if not returned, $60 applies (per [trident.gov.bb/registering/](https://trident.gov.bb/registering/)).

5. **Opening hours** — the EBC operates Monday to Friday, 8:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. Not on the page but useful contact information (per [EBC — Contact](https://www.ebc.gov.bb/contact/) and [EBC — Registration Information](https://www.ebc.gov.bb/registration-information/)).

6. **Email** — the EBC's public enquiry email is `ebcsupport@barbados.gov.bb`. Not on the page (per [EBC — Contact](https://www.ebc.gov.bb/contact/)).

7. **Processing time** — registration takes approximately 15 minutes; card collection takes within **eight weeks** after registration (per [trident.gov.bb/registering/](https://trident.gov.bb/registering/) — note: the previous report cited "12 weeks" which was incorrect; the current trident.gov.bb states "within eight weeks"). Not on the page.

8. **Religious-reasons exception for head coverings** — the EBC explicitly permits head coverings worn for religious reasons; this exception is absent from the page's photo guidelines.
