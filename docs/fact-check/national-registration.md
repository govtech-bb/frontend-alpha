# Fact-check: National registration

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/travel-id-citizenship/national-registration>
- **Source file:** `src/content/national-registration.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-28
- **Summary:** 12 claims reviewed — 4 verified, 4 discrepant, 4 unverifiable. Average certainty: **71%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **Governing Act is wrong — the Statistics Act is no longer the framework.** The page implies registration is governed by the Statistics Act, Cap. 192 (the old law). The Barbados Identity Management Act 2021 replaced it and is now the controlling statute. This affects legal references across the page and should be added explicitly.

2. **Photo requirements are materially incomplete.** The page lists only two prohibited items (head coverings and tinted glasses). The Electoral and Boundaries Commission's current registration guidance adds two further restrictions — no sleeveless garments (shoulders must be covered) and no white clothing (the background is white). Citizens arriving without these cues may have their photo rejected.

3. **Replacement card fee is correct ($60) but context is thin.** The $60 figure matches the GIS/CBC-confirmed fee under the Barbados Identity Management Act. However, the page does not mention: (a) that the first-ever registration is free; (b) that a same-day expedited replacement is $100; (c) that persons 65 and over are exempt from the replacement fee. These omissions could mislead citizens.

4. **Fax number is unverified.** The EBC's own contact page and official website do not publish fax number (246) 535-4863 — it appears only on the gov.bb source page and in older directories. The fax line may have been discontinued.

5. **Agency name uses old form.** The page names "Electoral and Boundaries Commission" correctly, but the EBC now operates the "Trident" national ID programme under the Barbados Identity Management Act 2021 rather than the old National Registration system. The page text ("National Registration Number") uses terminology from the superseded Act; "Barbados Identity Management" or "Trident ID" is the current programme name.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

1. **First card is free** — the EBC's current fee schedule confirms there is no charge for a first-time registration/card issuance. The page only mentions the $60 replacement fee; omitting the "free for first registration" fact could cause citizens to hesitate or expect a charge.

2. **Same-day expedited service** — replacement cards can be obtained same-day for $100 (confirmed by [EBC — Registration Information](https://www.ebc.gov.bb/registration-information/) and [Barbados Digital ID](https://trident.gov.bb/registering/)).

3. **Age-65+ exemption from replacement fee** — persons aged 65 and over are exempt from the $60 replacement fee (confirmed by [EBC — Registration Information](https://www.ebc.gov.bb/registration-information/) — "fee waived for persons 65 years and over").

4. **16-year-olds converting from minor to adult card** — if the old minor card is returned, conversion to an adult card is free; if not returned, $60 applies (per [EBC — Registration](https://www.ebc.gov.bb/registration-2/)).

5. **Opening hours** — the EBC operates Monday to Friday, 8:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. This is not on the page but is useful contact information.

6. **Email** — the EBC's public enquiry email is `ebcsupport@barbados.gov.bb`. Not on the page.

7. **Processing time** — registration takes approximately 15 minutes; card collection takes within 12 weeks (per [Barbados Digital ID — Registering](https://trident.gov.bb/registering/)). Not on the page.
