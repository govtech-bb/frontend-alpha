# Fact-check: Government requirements

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/business-trade/government-requirements>
- **Source file:** `src/content/government-requirements.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-29
- **Summary:** 12 claims reviewed — 6 verified, 2 discrepant, 4 unverifiable. Average certainty: **66%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **Entire page describes a repealed regulatory regime.** The International Financial Services Act, Cap 325 (IFSA) — the implicit statutory basis for every claim on this page — was repealed effective 1 January 2019. Former IFSC licensees are now "foreign currency earning banks" (FCBs) regulated under Part IIIB of the Financial Institutions (Amendment) Act, 2018-51 (FIA). The page uses the term "IFSC" and IFSA-era language without any indication the framework changed over six years ago. A business operator reading this today could act on a defunct regulatory pathway. The entire page needs a legal review and update against the FIA.

2. **"Annual returns to be filed by January 31" is discrepant.** The previous audit pass marked this as verified at 88%, but CAIPO's official website and the Companies Act, Cap. 308 s.15A show the annual return deadline is **not** January 31. It depends on incorporation date: companies incorporated January–June must file by **June 30**; those incorporated July–December must file by **December 31**. This is a material error citizens would rely on for compliance.

3. **"Licence renewed annually by January 1" — partially corroborated but under repealed law.** The Central Bank of Barbados confirms the licence fee is "statutorily due on January 1" — this supports the claim's January 1 date. However, the CBB's confirmation uses IFSA-era language. The operative renewal requirement under the current FIA Part IIIB has not been confirmed in a publicly accessible post-2018 statutory text.

4. **No enabling legislation cited anywhere on the page.** The page never names the governing Act. Given the IFSA's repeal and replacement by the FIA, naming the current legislation is essential for compliance. The omission is now materially misleading.

5. **Capital thresholds: USD denomination unconfirmed under current law.** The US$2,000,000 / US$500,000 figures are consistently cited in pre-2019 sources and CBB transition materials. No publicly accessible text of Part IIIB of the current FIA has been confirmed to retain the USD denomination.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

1. **The IFSA was repealed in 2019.** The page should carry a clear notice that the regulatory framework it describes derives from the IFSA (Cap 325), which was repealed effective 1 January 2019. Former IFSC licensees are now "foreign currency earning banks" regulated under Part IIIB of the Financial Institutions Act, Cap 324A as amended by the Financial Institutions (Amendment) Act, 2018-51. Source: [Central Bank of Barbados — IFSA Cap 325 press release](https://www.centralbank.org.bb/news/general-press-release/international-financial-services-act-cap-325-ifsa).

2. **Annual return deadline is based on incorporation date, not January 31.** The CAIPO website and Companies Act, Cap. 308 s.15A confirm: companies incorporated January–June file by June 30; companies incorporated July–December file by December 31. The page's "January 31" date is unsupported by authoritative sources. Source: [CAIPO — Post-incorporation requirements](https://caipo.gov.bb/corporate-affairs/post-incorporation/).

3. **The tax return filing deadline depends on fiscal year-end.** The page says tax returns must be filed with BRA but gives no deadline. Per BRA guidance: year-ends between January 1–September 30 → file by March 15 of the following year; year-ends between October 1–December 31 → file by June 15. Source: [BRA — Filing and Payment](https://bra.gov.bb/Popular-Topics/Companies/Filing-and-Payment).

4. **The governing legislation should be cited.** The page should name the Financial Institutions Act, Cap 324A (as amended) and reference the Central Bank of Barbados as the supervisory authority, with a link to centralbank.org.bb for current guidance.
