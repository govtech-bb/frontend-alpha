# Fact-check: Government requirements

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/business-trade/government-requirements>
- **Source file:** `src/content/government-requirements.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-28
- **Summary:** 12 claims reviewed — 7 verified, 1 discrepant, 4 unverifiable. Average certainty: **68%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **Entire page describes a repealed regulatory regime.** The International Financial Services Act, Cap 325 (IFSA) — which is the implicit statutory basis for every claim on this page — was repealed effective 1 January 2019. Former IFSC licensees are now "foreign currency earning banks" (FCBs) regulated under Part IIIB of the Financial Institutions (Amendment) Act, 2018-51 (FIA). The page uses the term "IFSC" and IFSA-era language without any indication that the framework changed over six years ago. A business operator reading this today could act on a defunct regulatory pathway. The entire page needs a legal review and update against the FIA.

2. **"Licence renewed annually by January 1" — cannot be confirmed under current law.** The January 1 renewal date appears on the gov.bb source page and derives from the IFSA; however, the FSC's 2026 annual licensing notice sets January 31 as the deadline for its regulated entities. The CBB (which supervises FCBs) has not published a publicly accessible post-2018 confirmation that January 1 is still the operative renewal deadline. The claim is unverifiable under current law.

3. **No enabling legislation cited anywhere on the page.** The page never names the governing Act. Given the IFSA's repeal and replacement by the FIA, naming the current legislation is essential for compliance. The omission was already a gap before the 2019 repeal; it is now materially misleading.

4. **Capital thresholds corroborated but denominated in USD — currency basis under the current Act is unconfirmed.** The US$2,000,000 / US$500,000 figures appear consistently in pre-2019 sources and CBB transition-era materials. An older IFSA text (silo.tips) cited Barbados dollar equivalents ($4,000,000 / $1,000,000), suggesting amounts were at some point adjusted or re-denominated. No publicly accessible text of Part IIIB of the current FIA has been confirmed to retain the USD denomination.

5. **The Ministerial consent step at incorporation is unverifiable under the current Act.** Under the FIA, the Central Bank as a corporate body is the licensing authority; whether a separate Ministerial consent at incorporation stage persists is not confirmed in any post-2018 primary source.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

1. **The IFSA was repealed in 2019.** The page should carry a clear notice that the regulatory framework it describes derives from the IFSA (Cap 325), which was repealed effective 1 January 2019. Former IFSC licensees are now "foreign currency earning banks" regulated under Part IIIB of the Financial Institutions Act, Cap 324A as amended by the Financial Institutions (Amendment) Act, 2018-51. Source: [Central Bank of Barbados — IFSA Cap 325 press release](https://www.centralbank.org.bb/news/general-press-release/international-financial-services-act-cap-325-ifsa).

2. **The tax return filing deadline depends on fiscal year-end.** The page says tax returns must be filed with BRA but gives no deadline. Per BRA guidance: year-ends between January 1–September 30 → file by March 15 of the following year; year-ends between October 1–December 31 → file by June 15. Source: [BRA — Filing and Payment](https://bra.gov.bb/Popular-Topics/Companies/Filing-and-Payment).

3. **The governing legislation should be cited.** The page should name the Financial Institutions Act, Cap 324A (as amended) and reference the Central Bank of Barbados as the supervisory authority, with a link to centralbank.org.bb for current guidance.
