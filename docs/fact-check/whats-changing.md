# Fact-check: What's changing?

- **Live page:** <https://alpha.gov.bb/whats-changing>
- **Source file:** `src/content/whats-changing.md`
- **Last checked:** 2026-05-28
- **Summary:** 10 claims reviewed — 7 verified, 0 discrepant, 3 unverifiable. Average certainty: **79%**.

---

## Headline issues for triage

1. **"GovTech" without qualifier (line 8).** The page refers to "GovTech" without a full name. The entity's legal name is "GovTech Barbados Ltd." and it consistently brands itself "GovTech Barbados" in press releases and its own communications. The short form "GovTech" is used colloquially but not in any official government statutory instrument or GIS press release. This is a minor branding/precision issue, not a factual error; the operator is identifiable, and the risk is low. No F-number raised.

2. **`publish_date: 2025-09-25` pre-dates the public launch of alpha.gov.bb (Claim 10).** Alpha.gov.bb was publicly launched and covered in press on 18 October 2025. A frontmatter date of 25 September 2025 is plausible as a CMS drafting date, but it cannot be independently corroborated. This is internal metadata not displayed to citizens, so citizen impact is nil.

3. **No major factual errors found in this pass.** The page is a high-level mission/vision statement. Its five bullet-point commitments are all substantiated by Tier 2–3 sources (GovTech Barbados blog, Barbados Today, CBC, Caribbean News Global). The specific design language ("user-centred service design", "clearer, simpler and faster", "designing inclusively", etc.) matches GovTech Barbados's published communications closely enough to be verified at the 85–90% band. The data-security commitment bullet is an internal platform promise that cannot be externally corroborated and is flagged as unverifiable.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

1. **No operator attribution.** Like the "What we mean by alpha" page, this page makes no mention of GovTech Barbados Ltd. as the site's operator. The page uses the informal "GovTech" without explanation. Citizens unfamiliar with the initiative have no indication of the responsible organisation. This is consistent with the pattern flagged at [terms-conditions.md](/home/gavin/frontend-alpha/docs/fact-check/terms-conditions.md) Claim 1 (F-091) — the Terms & Conditions page names an entity, but with the wrong ministry name (F-090).

2. **Homepage copy differs slightly.** The alpha.gov.bb homepage (`page.tsx` line 50) uses "How you find and use government services is changing", whereas this page (line 6) says "How you find and use public services is changing." The phrase "government services" vs "public services" is a minor inconsistency between the homepage banner and this sub-page. Neither is factually wrong, but consistency would strengthen the brand.

3. **No link to Terms & Conditions or privacy information.** The bullet on data security ("feel confident that their data and personal information is secure") is the most substantive commitment on this page, but there is no link to `alpha.gov.bb/terms-conditions` where the data handling policy is explained. See Claim 8 open question for the unresolved DPIA gap.
