## Headline issues for triage

1. **`publish_date: 2025-09-25` IS rendered citizen-facing (Claim 10 — status revised).** The previous pass incorrectly recorded this as "internal metadata not displayed to citizens." Live-checking the page on 2026-05-29 shows it renders as "Last Updated: September 25th, 2025" in the page body. The date pre-dates the public launch of alpha.gov.bb (widely reported 18 October 2025), so the displayed "Last Updated" date is misleading: it looks like the page was last reviewed on 25 September 2025 before the site was even public. Citizen impact: LOW-MEDIUM — users may assume the page is stale or wonder why a government service page was "last updated" before the site launched.

2. **Homepage copy differs from this page.** The alpha.gov.bb homepage hero reads "How you find and use **government** services is changing", while this page reads "How you find and use **public** services is changing." Neither is factually wrong, but the inconsistency undermines brand coherence.

3. **No link to Terms & Conditions or privacy notice from the data-security bullet.** The bullet "feel confident that their data and personal information is secure and only being used when strictly necessary and with their permission" is an unverifiable platform commitment. The Terms & Conditions page at `/terms-conditions` discloses the actual data-handling policy (AWS hosting in the US, Data Protection Act 2019) but this page does not link to it, leaving citizens without an auditable basis for the claim.

4. **No major factual errors.** The five bullet-point commitments are all substantiated by Tier 2–3 sources (GovTech Barbados blog, Barbados Today, CBC, Caribbean News Global). The data-security commitment remains unverifiable as an implemented platform policy. All footer and navigation links resolved on 2026-05-29.

## Additional findings (not on the page but should be)

1. **No operator attribution.** Like the "What we mean by alpha" page, this page makes no mention of GovTech Barbados Ltd. as the site's operator. The page uses the informal "GovTech" without explanation. Citizens unfamiliar with the initiative have no indication of the responsible organisation.

2. **Homepage copy differs slightly.** The alpha.gov.bb homepage hero reads "How you find and use **government** services is changing", whereas this page (line 6) says "How you find and use **public** services is changing." Neither is factually wrong, but the inconsistency between the homepage banner and this sub-page undermines brand coherence.

3. **No link to Terms & Conditions or privacy information from data-security bullet.** The bullet on data security (Claim 8) is the most substantive commitment on this page, but there is no link to `alpha.gov.bb/terms-conditions` where the data handling policy is explained (AWS hosting, DPA 2019 governance). Citizens relying on this page for assurance have no click-path to the policy document.

4. **`publish_date` renders as "Last Updated" citizen-facing.** The `publish_date: 2025-09-25` frontmatter field is rendered as "Last Updated: September 25th, 2025" in the page body. This was missed in the previous pass. The date predates the site's public launch (18 October 2025) and may confuse citizens. GovBB team should review whether `publish_date` is intended to represent the content review date and update accordingly.
