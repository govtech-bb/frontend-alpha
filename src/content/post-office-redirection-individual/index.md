You should use this service to tell the Post Office that you would like to change the address that your mail gets delivered to.

If you are 18 years old or over, you can complete a redirection notice to redirect mail addressed to you. You should also use this service to redirect the post of your dependents who are 16 years old and under.

A common use case is if you and your family are moving house.

A redirection notice will last for 6 months.

There are a separate forms if you would like to:

- [redirect your business mail](/travel-id-citizenship/post-office-redirection-business)
- [tell the Post Office that someone has died](/travel-id-citizenship/post-office-redirection-deceased)

## How to redirect personal mail

### Complete the form

There are 2 ways to redirect your personal mail. You can:

1. #### Tell the Post Office online

   You can complete the redirection form online. You must pay $4 BBD online as part of the process so you will need a debit or credit card, and you will need to have (or create) an EZPay+ account.

   <a data-start-link href="/travel-id-citizenship/post-office-redirection-individual/start">Complete the online form</a>

2. #### Get a paper redirection form any Post Office
   You must complete it by hand and return it to any Post Office.

## What you will need to share

Along with your personal details, the redirection form asks you to share:

- the name and National Identification number of the person applying
- name(s) of every other person who also wants to redirect their mail
- the old address
- the new address
- start date of redirection
- end date if the redirection is only temporary

## After you tell the Post Office

The Barbados Postal Service will begin to deliver your mail to your new address from the start date you gave on the form. Your redirection notice will last for 6 months.

The Post Office is responsible for this service.
