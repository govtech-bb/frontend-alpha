The Senate comprises 21 members. These members are appointed by the following process:

- **12** appointed by the Prime Minister
- **7** appointed by the Governor-General at his discretion to represent various interests in the community, such as religious, economic, social or other interests he thinks ought to be represented
- **2** appointed by the Leader of the Opposition

Senators may also be appointed as Ministers or Parliamentary Secretaries. The Senate meets chiefly when there is business from the House of Assembly.

The Presiding Officer of the Senate is the President. The President sometimes performs the functions of the office of Governor-General when the Governor-General is away from his post.

**The Senate is referred to as the Upper House.**

## Highlights of the Senate

The Senate Chamber features a fine oval mahogany table which is part of the original furniture of the Chamber.

The stained-glass windows in the Senate feature the Coats of Arms of former Speakers of the House and Presidents of the Legislative Council which preceded the Senate.

The chandeliers are made of Waterford Crystal and were a joint presentation by Lord Wedgwood of Barlston, England, and Mr. Geoffrey Cave of Cave Shepherd to the people of Barbados for the 350th Anniversary of Parliament in 1989.
