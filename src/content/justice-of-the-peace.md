A Justice of the Peace (JP) is appointed by the President of Barbados to help people with official documents — certifying copies, witnessing signatures, and taking sworn statements and affidavits. JPs are ordinary community members and their help is **free of charge**. They are not allowed to charge you.

## What a JP can do

- Certify a copy of a document, like a passport, birth certificate or driving licence
- Witness sworn statements and affidavits
- Witness your signature on legal papers, like consent forms

## What a JP cannot do

- Give legal advice or speak for you in court
- Certify a copy without seeing the original document
- Sign a document they have a personal interest in
- Charge you a fee
- Stamp documents for use in another country — you need a notary public for that (see [If you need a notary public](#if-you-need-a-notary-public))

## If you need a notary public

A notary public is a lawyer or chartered accountant with additional powers granted by the Government to certify documents for use outside Barbados. A JP cannot do this work — you need a notary.

For the official process, fees, and where to go, see [Get a document notarised](/travel-id-citizenship/get-a-document-notarised).

## What to bring

- The original document and the copy you want certified — the JP needs to see both
- A photo ID (passport, national ID card or driving licence)
- Any other documents the organisation has asked for

If you are signing something in front of the JP, **do not sign it before you arrive**.

   **Barbados Judicial System**, for JP services linked to court matters.

   Phone [(246) 535-9700](tel:+12465359700).

## If you live overseas

If you are outside Barbados and a document needs to be certified, you can usually use a **notary public** or commissioner of oaths in the country where you are. The certified document will be accepted in most cases.

For matters that specifically require a Barbados JP, contact the nearest Barbados High Commission or Consulate, or call the Office of the Attorney General on [+1 246 467-7370](tel:+12464677370) for advice.

## Where this information comes from

The directory uses the Supreme Court of Barbados register (last updated 30 April 2023), the [official 2024 list (PDF, 436 names)](/justices-of-the-peace-2024.pdf) and the 563 JPs appointed in January 2026. Every appointment is gazetted in the [Official Gazette of Barbados](https://governmentprintery.gov.bb/gazette/). To check whether someone is currently a JP, phone the Office of the Attorney General on [(246) 467-7370](tel:+12464677370).
