## Retirement ages — compulsory (MPS authoritative table)

| Period | Compulsory age |
|---|---|
| 1 Jan 2006 – 31 Dec 2009 | 65½ |
| 1 Jan 2010 – 31 Dec 2013 | 66 |
| 1 Jan 2014 – 31 Dec 2017 | 66½ |
| 1 Jan 2018 and thereafter | 67 |

**Note:** Treasury "Considerations" page omits the 2006–2009 row. MPS is the definitive authority.

## Retirement ages — voluntary

- Permanently appointed **on or before 15 July 1985**: voluntary retirement at **55**.
- Permanently appointed **after 15 July 1985**: voluntary retirement at **60**.

**Critical boundary:** the date 15 July 1985 itself belongs in the age-55 group ("on or before"). Any table that puts it in the age-60 group ("on or after") is wrong.

## PAD — dissolved January 2019

The Personnel Administration Division (PAD) was absorbed into the Ministry of Public Service in January 2019 (merger of Ministry of the Civil Service + PAD + Training Administration Division). The successor unit is:

**Ministry of Public Service — People Resourcing and Compliance Directorate**
1st Floor E. Humphrey Walcott Building, Cnr. Collymore Rock & Culloden Road, St. Michael
Tel. (246) 535-4500

Any alpha.gov.bb content that refers citizens to "the PAD" is using a defunct entity name.

## NIS abatement rule

The Pensions (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1975-31 applies abatement to officers entering service **after 1 September 1975**. For those officers, if eligible for both NIS pension and government pension, only the higher is paid. Officers who entered before 1 September 1975 are not subject to abatement (negligible number of current retirees).

## Temporary continuous workers

- 10 years or fewer + leaving during that period → **no pension** (Treasury verbatim: "will not receive a pension")
- 10+ years + retired in that state → pension **only if** the post they held was established and vacant
- Separately: temporary workers with at least 2 years whose service was terminated → eligible for a **lump sum compensation** (not a pension)

## Minimum service

Minimum 10 years pensionable service to qualify for any pension. No-pay leave is deducted from pensionable service total.

## Pension formula (from Treasury)

- Full Pension = (total pensionable months / 600) × last annual salary
- Reduced Pension = Full Pension × 0.75
- Gratuity = (Full Pension / 4) × 12.5
