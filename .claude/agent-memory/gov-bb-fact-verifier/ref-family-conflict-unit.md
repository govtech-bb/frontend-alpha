## Family Conflict Unit (FCU) — Royal Barbados Police Service

- **Official name:** "Family Conflict Unit" (also referred to as "Family Conflict Intervention Unit / FCIU" in some sources)
- **Established:** June 2013
- **Part of:** Royal Barbados Police Service (specialised unit)
- **Location:** Old Black Rock Police Station, Black Rock, St. Michael (NOT "Black Rock Police Station Annex")
- **Verified phones:** 430-7316 / 430-7323 (Mental Health Services Directory, fliphtml5.com); 430-7328 (Barbados Police Service Facebook)
- **Unverified numbers on alpha.gov.bb:** 845-0623 (actually a BPW number); 836-5070 (no source found — potentially dead/wrong)

**Why:** Phone numbers on the domestic abuse page were found to be mislabelled — 845-0623 is a BPW WhatsApp line; 836-5070 has no authoritative source. Life-safety issue.

**How to apply:** When verifying domestic violence pages, always cross-check FCU numbers against the Mental Health Services Directory and RBPF social media before trusting content files.

## BPW Crisis Centre and Shelter (NGO — not a government hotline)

- **Organisation:** Barbados Professional Women (BPW), non-governmental
- **Crisis hotline:** (246) 435-8222 (24-hour, also accepts WhatsApp/text)
- **Secondary (WhatsApp/calls):** (246) 845-0623 / (246) 253-5071
- **FCIU liaison number listed on BPW page:** (246) 430-7323
- **Email:** bpwbarbadosonline@gmail.com
- **Address:** P.O. Box 381, Bridgetown, Barbados, W.I.
- **Services:** counselling, safe shelter, referrals to health and legal services
- **Source:** [bpwbarbados.wordpress.com](https://bpwbarbados.wordpress.com/bpw-crisis-centre-and-shelter/)

**Why:** alpha.gov.bb labels 435-8222 as "the Crisis Hotline" implying a government service. It is an NGO line. The label discrepancy was flagged as F-042.

## Governing legislation

- **Domestic Violence (Protection Orders) Act, CAP. 130A** (L.R.O. 1993, as amended by 2016-2 Amendment Act)
- Emergency protection orders: valid up to 7 days from date of issue
- Civil protection orders: duration set by court, not exceeding 12 months
- Application for civil protection order: Form 1 (in Schedule to the Act)
- "Form 7 / Victim Rights Form 7": not confirmed in any public source — flagged as F-045 for agency verification
- **Source:** [barbadosparliament-laws.com/en/showdoc/cs/130A](https://barbadosparliament-laws.com/en/showdoc/cs/130A); [Baker McKenzie Barbados Topic 4](https://resourcehub.bakermckenzie.com/en/resources/fighting-domestic-violence/north-and-central--america/barbados/topics/4-protection-for-domestic-violence-victims-and-relief-granted)
