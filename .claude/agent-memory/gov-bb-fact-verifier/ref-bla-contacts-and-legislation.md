## "Motor Vehicles Act, 1988" — WRONG legislation name

- **No Barbados statute** by this name exists in the consolidated laws (confirmed via OAG statutes list and Barbados Law Courts database).
- "Motor Vehicles Act, 1988" is **India's** Act No. 59 of 1988 (enacted by the Indian Parliament).
- The correct Barbados legislation governing driving licences is the **Road Traffic Act, CAP 295**, most recently consolidated to 2022.
- The alpha.gov.bb page (and the gov.bb source page) appear to have been templated from Indian government content — the phrasing "no person can drive a motor vehicle in any public place unless he holds a valid Driving License" matches Indian act language verbatim.
- Sources: https://www.barbadoslawcourts.gov.bb/assets/content/pdfs/statutes/RoadTrafficCAP295.pdf; https://mtw.gov.bb/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/Road-Traffic-Act-295-consolidated-up-to-2022-21-2022-12-23-1-1.pdf

## 30 days vs 3 months — learner permit to driving test

- **BLA's own Learner Permit page:** "you are required by law to practice for a minimum three (3) months before applying for the driving examination" (first-time holders)
- **gov.bb (and alpha.gov.bb):** "apply after 30 days and within 180 days of issue of the Learner's Permit"
- The 180-day upper bound is consistent with all sources.
- The 30-day lower bound appears to conflict with BLA's 3-month (90-day) requirement for first-time holders; may apply to permit renewals.
- The BLA is the authoritative licensing body — trust its 3-month requirement over gov.bb's 30-day figure.

## International Permit — document list

BLA page lists: valid driver licence; proof of identity; valid passport (with visa where necessary); itinerary listing countries to be visited; two passport photographs.
NOT listed: "attested copy of address proof" or "attested copy of birth certificate" — these appear on alpha.gov.bb and are Indian template artefacts.
