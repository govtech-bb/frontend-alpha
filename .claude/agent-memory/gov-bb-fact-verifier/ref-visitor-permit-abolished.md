Visitor driving permits were abolished effective **15 October 2025** by the Barbados Revenue Authority.

- Visitors may now drive in Barbados on a **valid home-country driving licence** for the class of vehicle they intend to drive — no permit required.
- A **Car Rental Levy (BBD $5.00 per day, maximum $35.00 per rental contract)** replaced the permit. It is collected by car rental companies and private operators at the point of hire, itemised on receipts, and remitted to BRA monthly via TAMIS. Visitors do not apply for it separately.
- Levy proceeds fund the Barbados Oceans and Coastal Authority and the Barbados Nature Conservancy Trust.
- **CARICOM nationals** from Belize, Dominica, and Saint Vincent and the Grenadines exercising free-movement rights may drive on their foreign licence for up to 12 months before exchanging for a local licence.
- The BLA service page (bla.gov.bb/servicedetails/VmlzaXRvciBQZXJtaXQ=) had **not yet been updated** as of 2026-05-28 and still shows the old permit as active. The BRA policy note is the governing authority.
- Pre-abolishment fees (for historical reference): under 2 months = BBD $10; 2 months–1 year = BBD $100.
