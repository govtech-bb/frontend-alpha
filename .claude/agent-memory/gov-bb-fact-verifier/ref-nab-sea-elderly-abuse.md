## National Assistance Board / SEA — elder abuse reporting

**Canonical address:** Murrell House, Country Road, St. Michael (NOT "Country Rd, Bridgetown, Saint Michael")
- All three Tier 1/2 sources (gov.bb, connectb1m.com, centenariansofbarbados.com) lead with "Murrell House" as the building name.
- "Bridgetown" does not appear in any authoritative source for this address.

**Phone:** (246) 535-3131 (PBX — Murrell House building; confirmed by gov.bb, connectb1m.com, centenariansofbarbados.com; building now operates as SEA location)

**Email:** `nab.department@barbados.gov.bb` — UNVERIFIED. Not published on gov.bb NAB page, GIS releases, NAB Facebook page, or any third-party directory. If this address is unconfirmed with the SEA, remove it from the page — crisis pages must not carry unverified contact emails.

**Agency name:** The National Assistance Board (NAB) was dissolved 2 January 2026 and merged into the Social Empowerment Agency (SEA). The SEA also absorbed: Welfare Department, Child Care Board, National Disabilities Unit, Resilience and Reintegration Unit. Pages referencing "National Assistance Board" are now stale.

**Ministry:** Ministry of People Empowerment and Elder Affairs (MPEA), Minister Adrian Forde (as of April 2026).

**Older Persons (Care and Protection) Act, 2026:**
- Debated House of Assembly April 2026; Senate backing reported May 2026; Royal Assent/commencement date unknown as of 2026-05-28.
- Once commenced: SEA becomes the statutory body for elder abuse reports, maintains a national Register of Abuse, has emergency removal powers.
- Mandatory reporting by professionals (healthcare, social workers, law enforcement); failure = $10,000 fine.
- Anonymous reporting permitted — receives same attention as named reports.
- Penalties for elder abuse: $100,000 fine and/or 5 years imprisonment.
- Page should be updated once commencement is announced.

**gov.bb page currency:** gov.bb/Ministries/social-care still lists the NAB as a department (stale — not yet updated to reflect the merger).
