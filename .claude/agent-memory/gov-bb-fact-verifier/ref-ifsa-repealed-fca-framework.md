The International Financial Services Act, Cap 325 (IFSA) was repealed effective 1 January 2019. Entities previously licensed as IFSCs (International Financial Services Companies) are now known as "foreign currency earning banks" (FCBs) and fall under Part IIIB of the Financial Institutions (Amendment) Act, 2018-51 (FIA Amendment), which amended the Financial Institutions Act, Cap 324A.

**Capital requirements (confirmed across multiple sources):**
- US$2,000,000 minimum assigned capital if authorised to accept third-party deposits
- US$500,000 minimum assigned capital if not accepting third-party deposits
- Minister may alter these sums at discretion

**Annual requirements (confirmed from gov.bb source and original IFSA):**
- Licence renewed annually by January 1 (NB: FSC sets January 31 for its own entities — different regulator)
- Annual audit required
- Annual returns to CAIPO by January 31 (Companies Act, Cap 308, s.15A)
- Annual filing of audited financial statements
- Quarterly statements of assets and liabilities to the Central Bank

**Tax filing:** Annual returns to Barbados Revenue Authority; deadline depends on fiscal year-end (March 15 for Jan–Sep year-ends; June 15 for Oct–Dec year-ends).

**Supervisory authority:** Central Bank of Barbados (Bank Supervision Department); Director of Bank Supervision is referenced in the licensing process.
