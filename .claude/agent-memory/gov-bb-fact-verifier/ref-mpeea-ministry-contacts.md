## Key discrepancies in ministries.ts (lines 833–915)

1. **Three dissolved bodies still listed as associated departments:**
   - "The National Assistance Board", "The Child Care Board", "Welfare Department" — all dissolved 2 January 2026 → Social Empowerment Agency (SEA).
   - "National Disabilities Unit" also dissolved into SEA but is listed as if still standalone.
   - Resilience and Reintegration Unit (also merged into SEA) is NOT listed at all.
   - Correct: list "Social Empowerment Agency (SEA)" as the successor to all five bodies.

2. **Community Development Department wrongly attributed to MPEEA:**
   - CDD sits under Ministry of Youth, Sports and Community Empowerment (MYSCE).
   - comdev.gov.bb says "Ministry of Sports and Community Empowerment"; MYSCE's mysce.gov.bb confirms CDD under their mandate.
   - March 2026 GIS article: MYSCE minister Griffith presided over a CDD-hosted seminar.
   - CDD contacts: PBX 535-1650/1655/1656; FAX 535-1693; Email: comdev.barbados@barbados.gov.bb; Address: 4th Floor Warrens Office Complex (same building as MPEEA).

3. **socialcare.gov.bb is a "Coming Soon" placeholder** — not a live ministry website. Should be removed or replaced with gov.bb/Ministries/social-care.

## SEA merger (2 January 2026)
Five bodies merged: Welfare Department + Child Care Board + National Assistance Board + National Disabilities Unit + Resilience and Reintegration Unit.
Sources: Barbados Today (7 Jan 2026); Caribbean News Global (8 Jan 2026).
SEA client centres: Six Roads St Philip (7 Jan 2026), Southern Plaza Oistins (27 Mar 2026), 11 more planned.

## Older Persons (Care and Protection) Bill 2026
In Senate debate as of 7 May 2026. Not yet assented to. Will make SEA the statutory body for elder abuse reports.
