## Key facts established 2026-05-28

### Rate reform timeline
- **Pre-2024:** Standard rate was 25% (flat). Life insurance: 5%. Manufacturing: 15%. Residential rental: 15%.
- **Income year 2024:** Replaced with tiered rates — ≤$1M: 5.5%; $1M–$20M: 3%; $20M–$30M: 2.5%; >$30M: 1%.
- **Income year 2025+:** Standard flat rate 9% (all companies except below).
- **Approved small businesses** (gross income ≤ BBD $2M, registered under Small Business Development Act): 5.5%.
- **Qualifying IP income (patent box regime):** 4.5%.
- **Insurance companies:** Class 1: 0%; Class 2: 2%; Class 3: 2%. Qualifying insurance companies (life, licensed before 17 Oct 2017): 0.35%. Exempt insurance: 0%.
- **MNE groups (top-up tax):** 15% minimum effective rate via Corporation Top-Up Tax Act 2024-16.

### Legislation
- Income Tax (Amendment and Validation) Act, 2024-15 — gazetted 24 May 2024, effective 1 January 2024.
- Corporation Top-Up Tax Act, 2024-16 — enacted May 2024, effective from fiscal years beginning on or after 31 December 2023.

### Loss carry-forward changes
- Pre-2015: 9 years.
- Income years 2015–2024: 7 years.
- From income year 2025: 5 years.
- From income year 2019: losses may only offset 50% of taxable income in any given year (utilisation cap).
- Life insurance companies: cannot carry forward losses (under reformed regime).

### Group relief (reformed)
- Available from income year 2024 (for entities with pre-2024 losses >USD 50M) or income year 2025 (all others).
- Eligibility test: both companies Barbados-resident, subject to tax at 9%, meet 75% subsidiary test.
- Cap: relief may not exceed 50% of the tax otherwise payable.
- Former exclusions (IBC, EIC, SRL, offshore banks) are no longer applicable — those regimes were frozen for new entrants as of 31 December 2018.

### Premium taxes (unchanged by 2024 reform)
- Life insurance, new business: 6% of gross direct premium income.
- Resident life insurance, renewal: 3%.
- Non-resident life insurance, renewal: 5%.
- Property insurance: 4.75%.
- Other general insurance: 4%.

### Capital allowances (largely unchanged)
- Industrial buildings annual allowance: 4%.
- IP: 50% of cost over 10 years.
- Manufacturing: 150% of cost.
- Annual allowances range: 5%–33⅓% (straight-line).
- Equipment initial allowance: 20% (per gov.bb/tax-information, not independently confirmed from BRA).
- Industrial buildings initial allowance: 40% (same caveat).
