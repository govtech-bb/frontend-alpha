## Child Care Board / Social Empowerment Agency — Day Nurseries

**Governing legislation:** Child Care Board Act, Cap. 381 (Laws of Barbados); Child Care Board Regulations 1985 (Cap. 381'B); Child Care Board (Private Child Care Centres) Regulations, 1986 (Cap. 381'A)

**Primary website:** childcareboard.gov.bb (live as of 2026-05-28)
**gov.bb listing:** https://www.gov.bb/State-Bodies/child-care-board

**CCB contact details (confirmed 2026-05-28):**
- Address: Fred Edgehill Building, Cheapside, Bridgetown, Barbados (also rendered "Cheapside Road, St. Michael" on gov.bb)
- Phone General Office: (246) 535-2800
- Phone Chairman: (246) 535-2827
- Phone Director: (246) 535-2842
- Fax: (246) 435-3172
- Email: childcareboard@barbados.gov.bb
- Hours: Monday–Friday 8:30 AM – 4:30 PM

**Ministry responsible:** Ministry of People Empowerment and Elder Affairs
- Minister: The Hon. Adrian R. Forde, M.P.
- The CCB is listed under this ministry's associated departments (src/data/ministries.ts lines 834–915)

**SEA merger (critical governance change):**
As of 2 January 2026, the Child Care Board was merged into the newly established Social Empowerment Agency (SEA), which also absorbed the Welfare Department, National Assistance Board, National Disabilities Unit, and the Resilience and Reintegration Unit. The SEA operates under the Ministry of People Empowerment and Elder Affairs (Minister: Adrian Forde). The CCB website and gov.bb listing remain live as of 2026-05-28. Whether day nursery applications are now processed under the SEA name or continue under the CCB brand is unconfirmed from public sources.
- Source: Barbados Today 2026-01-07, 2026-03-27; socialempowermentbb.org (ECONNREFUSED on 2026-05-28)

**Day nursery age range (confirmed from secondary sources):**
Children between the ages of 3 months and 5 years (Barbados Children Directory citing CCB). The primary source childcareboard.gov.bb/daycare/ does not publish this age range in its web content.

**Number of CCB day care centres:** 15 (confirmed childcareboard.gov.bb/daycare/ and secondary sources)

**Application process:** NOT documented on any public Tier 1 source (childcareboard.gov.bb, gov.bb, GIS). The alpha.gov.bb page describes a procedure (visit each nursery, supervisor completes form, senior officer checks eligibility, orientation session before starting) — none of this is confirmed from public sources. All procedural claims on the alpha page are unverifiable.

**Green Book:** No gov.bb source uses this term. Barbados polyclinics issue immunisation/health cards to caregivers; the term "Green Book" appears to be informal usage. Whether CCB formally calls it a "Green Book" is unconfirmed.

**Universal nursery expansion (March 2026):** Ministry of Education Transformation announced every 3- and 4-year-old will have a guaranteed nursery place from September 2026. This is a Ministry of Education programme (nursery schools, not CCB day care centres) — distinct from CCB services but overlaps the same age group.
- Source: Barbados Today 2026-03-03

**GIS tag page:** https://gisbarbados.gov.bb/blog/tag/child-care-board/ — returned HTTP 403 on 2026-05-28

**Child abuse reporting contact (confirmed 2026-05-28):**
- The CCB's own reporting page lists phone (246) 535-2800, fax (246) 435-3172, and email childcareboard@barbados.gov.bb as active contact channels. Online contact form also available at childcareboard.gov.bb.
- Anonymity is guaranteed to all reporting individuals (confirmed on childcareboard.gov.bb/reporting-child-abuse/).
- Referrals accepted from: victims themselves, schools, family members, doctors, police, and individuals with knowledge of an offence.

**New child protection legislation (April 2026):**
Three new child protection Acts proclaimed January 2026 took effect April 2026. Minister Adrian Forde confirmed mandatory reporting obligations for all persons "in the precinct of children." These Acts are not yet in full-text machine-readable form on the public web (PDF binary on UWI Cavehill). Reports are still made to the CCB/SEA, not a new agency.
- Source: Barbados Today 2026-04-24; CBC 2026-01-03

**Response-time standards (UNVERIFIABLE — do not publish without agency confirmation):**
No authoritative source publishes an "attend immediately" standard for serious danger cases or a "begin investigation within 24 hours" standard for children under 5. The 24-hour reference on childcareboard.gov.bb relates to the mandatory *reporting* obligation on professionals, not SEA/CCB response time. Do not repeat these claims without SEA confirmation.

**Address spelling note:** gov.bb uses "Fred Edghill" (one word, no 'e' before 'hill'); childcareboard.gov.bb uses "Fred Edgehill" (with 'e'). The gov.bb spelling "Edghill" should be treated as authoritative (as it matches the government's own data entry on the State Bodies page).
